GTA Artificial Turf Bylaw Guide (2026)

Is artificial grass legal in your city? We broke down the bylaws so you don't have to.

Disclaimer: Municipal bylaws change. While we update this page regularly, we always verify current codes before breaking ground on your project.

City of Toronto

STRICT BUT DOABLE

Backyards: Generally permitted without a permit, provided standard soft landscaping ratios are maintained (turf counts as soft landscaping if permeable).

Front Yards: Toronto has strictly enforced bylaws regarding front yard soft landscaping. The city categorizes artificial turf carefully. In many wards, a specific percentage of the front yard must be living plant material. We highly recommend artificial turf strictly for backyards and side yards in Toronto proper.

City of Vaughan

TURF FRIENDLY

Vaughan is one of the more progressive municipalities regarding artificial turf. It is widely accepted as a permeable soft-landscaping alternative in both front and backyards, provided it is installed professionally over a draining aggregate base.

Mississauga & Brampton

GENERALLY PERMITTED

Artificial turf is permitted. The key requirement from these municipalities relates to drainage. The installation must not alter the property's grading in a way that directs run-off onto adjacent properties. Our 5-inch crushed aggregate sub-base perfectly satisfies these drainage requirements.

Do I Need a Permit?

For a standard backyard installation replacing existing grass, no building permit is required. Permits are only necessary if we are building retaining walls over 1 metre high, altering city grading profiles, or working within highly protected conservation authority (TRCA/CVC) ravines.
