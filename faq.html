General Questions

Basic information about artificial turf

Modern artificial turf is made from high-quality synthetic fibers, typically polyethylene or polypropylene. These materials are designed to look and feel like natural grass while being incredibly durable.

The turf is constructed in layers: the grass blades are attached to a backing material with drainage holes, and infill (usually silica sand or rubber) is added to keep the blades upright and add weight.

Not anymore! Today's premium artificial turf is virtually indistinguishable from natural grass. We use turf with multiple blade shapes, colours, and textures that mimic real grass perfectly.

Most visitors won't realize it's artificial unless you tell them. Our design visualization preview lets you see exactly how realistic it will look in your yard before installation.

Artificial turf can get warm on hot summer days, similar to a deck or driveway. However, it's typically 10-15 degrees cooler than concrete surfaces.

A quick spray with the hose cools it down instantly. Many of our customers find that the turf cools quickly once the sun moves or in the evening, and their pets and kids still enjoy playing on it throughout the summer.

Artificial turf offers several environmental benefits:

  • Eliminates the need for watering (saves thousands of litres annually)
  • No gas-powered mowing or lawn equipment
  • No pesticides, herbicides, or fertilizers needed
  • Lasts 15-25 years before replacement

While synthetic materials are used, the water savings alone make it an eco-friendly choice for many homeowners.

Maintenance

Keeping your turf looking great

Artificial turf requires minimal maintenance compared to natural grass:

  • Monthly: Brush to keep blades upright (5 minutes)
  • As needed: Remove leaves and debris with a leaf blower
  • Occasionally: Rinse with water to remove dust
  • Annually: Inspect edges and seams

That's it! No mowing, watering, fertilizing, or pesticides needed.

Most spills can be cleaned with water and a mild soap solution:

  • Food/drinks: Rinse with water, use mild soap if needed
  • Grease/oil: Mild degreaser and water
  • Chewing gum: Freeze with ice, then scrape off
  • Paint: Mineral spirits or paint remover (test first)

For stubborn stains, we're always happy to provide guidance.

Municipal Rules & Bylaws

Know the rules for your city (Updated 2026)

Backyards: 100% allowed. No permits required for standard landscaping replacement.

Front Yards: Toronto zoning explicitly requires % of "soft landscaping".

  • The Rule: 75% of your front yard setback (excluding driveway) must be "soft landscaping".
  • The Good News: As of 2024, high-quality permeable artificial turf IS considered soft landscaping in many interpretations, provided it absorbs water.
  • Recommendation: We install permeable sub-bases that meet drainage requirements.

Mississauga: Permitted in front and back yards. Must maintain proper drainage (which our systems do).

Brampton: "Widening" driveways with turf is a common grey area. If you are replacing grass with turf, it is generally permitted. If you are paving over grass, you need a permit. Since turf is permeable, it usually bypasses the "paving" restriction.

Vaughan: Very turf-friendly. The city categorizes it as a "pervious surface" as long as it is installed on a draining base (not concrete).

Richmond Hill: Similar to Vaughan. No specific bans on artificial turf frontages.

For 95% of residential projects (replacing existing grass): NO permit is required.

You only technically need a permit if you are:

  • Altering the grade/slope of your land significantly
  • Building a retaining wall >1 metre high
  • Widening your driveway onto city property
